Bubble tea is known for its fun texture and customisable ingredients. Design your own following these steps!
1. Would you like a fruity tea or a milky tea?

Fruity bubble teas are made with fruit syrups and are typically lighter and more refreshing.
Milky bubble teas combine tea with milk or cream for a richer, creamier taste.
2. What tea would you like?

  • Black Tea
    A classic choice, known for its robust flavor and ability to stand up well to milk and sweeteners.

  • Green Tea
    Offers a lighter, more delicate flavor profile, often used in bubble tea variations like matcha or jasmine green tea.
3. Select your toppings!

Tapioca Pearls
A subtly sweet, chewy addition to your drink made from tapioca starch
Popping Boba
Tiny balls made from seaweed extract filled with fruit juice that burst when bitten into and come in a variety of flavours
Jelly
Made from coconut and adds texture to your drink. Comes in different flavours and shapes, including cubes, strips and stars
Pudding
made from egg yolks, cream, and sugar and is typically paired with the creamier, more indulgent milk teas
Aloe Vera
Adds a refreshing crunch and is known for its potential health benefits. It pairs beuatifully with lighter, fruitier teas
